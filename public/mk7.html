          Custom Formula Help

          Formula Coefficients

          Every combo is scored by multiplying each stat’s value by its coefficient and summing them all.

          A positive coefficient maximizes the stat, a negative one minimizes it, and zero means the stat is ignored.

          Speed and Handling

          The Speed and Handling stats are unified versions of their three component stats.

          Internally, the coefficient you set for these is split into the component stats as follows: 85% to ground, 10% to air and 5% to underwater.

          Filters and Locks

          You can set minimum and maximum values for each stat. Combos outside this range will be excluded.

          Note that your locks are respected, and may conflict with these filters.

          Help

          Combo Parts

          A combo is comprised of a driver, a body, wheels, and a glider.

          Tap any combo part to change it.

          Part Classes

          Parts that have the same stats as your current combo have a dashed outline in the part picker.

          Note that frame size can change between drivers of the same class.

          Locks

          You can lock a part to limit which combos appear in the Dominant, Similar and Custom Search tabs. This can help you find interesting combos according to your style preferences.

          Random Combo

          Tap the Random button to replace your currently active combo with random parts. Lock individual parts to keep them.

          Mini-Turbo (MTB)

          The hidden Mini-Turbo stat determines the length drift boosts.

          Speed (SPD)

          Speed has 3 stats, only the first of which is displayed in-game. They are Ground (SPDGR), Water (SPDWT) and Air (SPDAR). They determine your top speed in each medium.

          Acceleration (ACC)

          Acceleration determines how fast you can reach top speed.

          Weight (WGT)

          Weight determines the physics of collisions with other vehicles. Higher weights push lower weights.

          Handling (HND)

          Handling has 3 stats, only the first of which is displayed in-game. They are Ground (HNDGR), Water (HNDWT) and Air (HNDAR). They determine how sharp and wide you can drift and steer in each medium.

          Off-Road (OFF)

          Off-Road determines how much you slip and slow down on terrain like sand, snow, ice and wet asphalt.

          Stability (STB)

          Stability determines how much the tires stick to the ground.

          Drift (DFT)

          Drift determines how much you slip outwards when drifting.

          Frame Size

          The driver determines the combo’s frame size between small, medium, and large. This affects your in-game hitbox.
